Why I Built It

Accountability without another round of nagging.

It started as a way to show my own kids what needs to be done, what “done” actually means, and how approved work adds up over a real pay cycle. After a coworker asked to use it with her grandkids, I went back through every chore app I had tried and built the parent workflow I had been looking for.

Photo proof

Kids document completed work, and parents review the evidence before approving it.

Real feedback

Parents can approve work or send it back with instructions so kids can correct and resubmit it.

Pay cycles and points

Families choose the earning model and schedule that fits their household.

PTO and chore days

Parents control days off and whether chores happen on weekdays, every day, or custom days.

Who This Test Is For

A parent or guardian willing to try the real workflow.

This test works best for families with kids old enough to understand chores and use a phone or tablet camera. You may use fake names and a made-up household if you would rather explore without entering your family's information.

Please keep this invitation link within the invited testing group. Beta access is free, no payment card is required, and there is no payment or prize for participating. If you use real family information, avoid showing faces, addresses, school details, documents, or other sensitive information in proof photos.
Your 3–7 Day Assignment

Use it like a regular parent would.

You do not need to test every button. Complete this path and tell me where the experience gets unclear.

  1. Create a parent account and complete the household setup.
  2. Add at least one child and one chore. Try a template bundle if it fits.
  3. Open the kid dashboard link or QR code on another device and submit a chore with proof.
  4. Review the submission as the parent. Approve one item and send another back as Needs Fix if possible.
  5. Change one setting, such as chore days, pay cycle, approval mode, PTO, or compensation.
  6. Return over the next 3–7 days and note anything confusing, broken, unexpectedly slow, or different across devices.
What To Report

Confusing, broken, or weird all count.

Tell me what you expected, what happened instead, the device/browser you used, and the approximate time. Screenshots or quick screen recordings are especially helpful.
